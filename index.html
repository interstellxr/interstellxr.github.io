Research Focus Areas

Rocky Exoplanet Characterization

In October 2026, I will start my PhD at the CEA/LMD labs in Paris. There, I will be utilizing high-precision James Webb Space Telescope (JWST) observations to investigate the atmospheric compositions, if any, of rocky exoplanets.

Icy Moons & Outer Solar System Climates

At the moment I am completing my Master's thesis. I am working with a novel radiative-microphysical model to analyze Triton's atmosphere.

High-Resolution Spectroscopy

In autumn 2025 I had the pleasure of carrying out an internship at Geneva Observatory, where I worked on exoplanetary atmospheres via ground-based, high-resolution transmission spectroscopy. I gained experience in processing high-fidelity archival spectra (such as ESPRESSO data) to extract atomic lines like Lithium in the atmospheres of hot Jupiters.
